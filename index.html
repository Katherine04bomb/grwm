📶 SIM Card & eSIM · 手机卡
Stay connected from the moment you land
⚠️ Important: There are four options to use data in China. Expect using your international roaming, you also can...
📲 eSIM (Best Option)
  • Buy on Airalo, Holafly, or China Unicom International before flying
  • Activates the moment you land — no waiting at airport
  • Works on any eSIM-compatible phone
  • Best option if you want zero stress on arrival
🛬 Airport SIM
  • China Mobile and China Unicom counters at all major airports
  • Bring your passport — registration is required by law
  • Tourist SIMs: 30–90 days, usually unlimited data
  • Costs roughly ¥100–200 (about $14–28)
📡 Pocket WiFi
  • Rent at the airport or pre-book online
  • Great for sharing data with travel companions
  • Return at end of trip — refundable deposit
  • Good backup if SIM doesn't work for your phone
💡 Kat's tip: Get an eSIM online before you fly.
💳 Payments in China · 支付
China is almost entirely cashless — here's how to survive
The reality: Even tiny street food stalls only accept QR code payments. No Alipay or WeChat Pay = real trouble. My friend literally couldn't pay for his meal. Set this up at home.
💳
Alipay 支付宝 — Priority #1
Now officially supports foreign Visa/Mastercard/Amex directly. Download, register with your phone number, link your card, done. Used at literally everything — restaurants, taxis, markets, convenience stores.
✅ Foreigners: use the "International" version. Link your card in Settings → Bank Cards.
💬
WeChat Pay 微信支付
Built into WeChat. Also supports foreign cards now but the setup is slightly more complex. Worth setting up as a backup.
✅ You'll need WeChat anyway — set up the payment while you're at it.
💵
Cash (Backup)
Bring ¥500–1000 RMB as emergency backup. Some older vendors, temples, and rural areas still prefer cash. Exchange at Bank of China — best rates.
⚠️ Notify your home bank before travel to prevent card blocks at ATMs.
🌐 Firewall & VPN · 网络
The Great Firewall — what's blocked and how to stay connected
Why are apps blocked? China operates a national internet filter called the "Great Firewall." It's not a glitch — it's government policy. Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, YouTube, and most Western news sites are blocked.
🚫
What doesn't work in China
Google (Search, Maps, Gmail, YouTube), Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Twitter/X, Snapchat, most news websites, Spotify, Netflix, and many Western services.
⚠️ Even some hotel WiFis are filtered.
🔐
VPN — The Golden Rule
Download and TEST your VPN BEFORE flying. Once in China, the App Store and Play Store are filtered and you cannot easily download VPN apps. This is the #1 mistake first-timers make.
Free VPNs almost never work.
✅
What you can use instead
WeChat (messaging), Amap (maps & navigation), Dianping (restaurant reviews — China's Yelp).
✅ All Chinese apps work perfectly without VPN.
⚖️ Legal note: VPN use by tourists is a gray area. Millions of tourists use them every year without issue. Use for personal access only.
📱 Essential Apps · 必备APP
Download these 6 before you fly — they run your entire trip
💬
WeChat 微信 — Everything App
Messaging, payments, mini-programs, food ordering, maps. Without WeChat, China is very hard. Set it up before arrival and link a payment method.
✅ Register with your phone number. Add your Chinese friends so they can show you around.
💳
Alipay 支付宝 — Pay for Everything
China's dominant payment platform. Foreign cards now accepted. Street food, taxis, supermarkets — all QR code.
✅ Link your Visa/Mastercard in Settings. Do this BEFORE you arrive.
🚗
Didi 滴滴 — China's Uber
Works in English. No language barrier. Much cheaper than taxis. The driver comes to you — no hailing required.
✅ Set your pickup and destination in English, the app handles the translation.
🗺️
Amap 高德地图 — Navigation
Google Maps doesn't work well in China. Amap (Gaode) is the most accurate map — it has an English mode and works offline.
✅ Switch to English in Settings. Download your city maps offline before exploring.
🍜
大众点评 Dianping — Where to Eat
China's version of Yelp + TripAdvisor. Every restaurant, café, and attraction rated and reviewed. Filter by "foreigner-friendly" or "English menu".
✅ Use it to find the best local spots tourists never discover.
🚆
Trip.com (携程) — Book Everything
Trains, flights, hotels all in one English-language app. Much easier than the official 12306 rail app for foreigners. Supports foreign payment cards.
✅ Book high-speed rail here — it's the most reliable way as a foreigner.
🔤 For translation: Google Translate (with offline Chinese downloaded) works well for text and camera translation. Baidu Translate is a solid local alternative- it understands Chinese perfectly. Use Papago for photo translation (menu, signs).
🚄 Getting Around China · 交通
The world's best rail network, easy metro, and Didi for taxis
🚄 High-Speed Rail
  • World's best rail system — use it between cities!
  • Beijing → Shanghai: 4.5 hours (vs 1.5hr flight + 3hr airport process)
  • Book on Trip.com with your passport number
  • G trains = fastest, D trains = fast, C = city rail
  • Bring your passport to collect tickets at the station
🚇 Metro
  • Every major city has modern, cheap metro
  • Machines have English — easy to use
  • Or scan QR code in WeChat/Alipay at gates
  • ¥3–10 per journey — extremely affordable
  • Covers all tourist spots in major cities
🚗 Didi (Taxi App)
  • Like Uber — book in the app, no language needed
  • Show the destination text to driver if unsure
  • Very affordable: ¥20–40 for most city rides
  • Also: 曹操出行, 花小猪 (cheaper Didi alternatives)
  • Never use unlicensed taxis at airports!
✈️ Domestic Flights
  • Best for very long distances (Beijing → Yunnan)
  • Book on Trip.com — English support
  • Arrive 2 hours early — security is thorough
  • Budget airlines: Xiamen Air, Shenzhen Airlines
