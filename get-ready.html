Step 1
🛂 Check Your Passport
Is it still valid? China requires at least 6 months validity beyond your travel dates. No passport? Start the application now — it takes 4–6 weeks.
Step 2
📋 Sort Your Visa
Requirements depend on your nationality. Some countries are now visa-free for 15 days. Others need to apply. Click to find yours.
Step 3
✈️ Book Flights & Hotels
Once your visa is approved, book everything in advance. P.S. Not all hotels accept foreigners. International chains are always safe.
Step 4
📶 Get a SIM or eSIM
Many hotels need a Chinese phone number to check in and connect the wifi nowadays. Get an eSIM before you fly, or buy a China Mobile SIM at the airport with your passport.
Step 5
💳 Set Up Payments
China is almost entirely cashless. Street food stalls, taxis, supermarkets — all QR code. Link your foreign card to Alipay before you land. This is the #1 thing to do.
Step 6
🌐 Internet & Social Media
Google, Instagram, WhatsApp are blocked in China. Download a VPN before you fly would make you feel safe.
Step 7
📱 Download Your Apps
WeChat, Alipay, Didi, Amap, Dianping, Trip.com. These 6 apps will run your entire trip.
Step 8
🚄 Understand Transport
China's high-speed rail is the best in the world. Learn how to book trains, use Didi, and navigate the metro system.
Step 9
🛃 Arrival & Immigration
What to expect at Chinese immigration, health declaration forms, baggage rules, and how to get from the airport to your hotel smoothly.

📶 SIM Card & eSIM · 手机卡
Stay connected from the moment you land
⚠️ Important: Many Chinese hotels require a local phone number to complete check-in. Getting a SIM is a good option.
📲 eSIM (Best Option)
  • Buy on Airalo before flying — activates on landing
  • Works on any eSIM-compatible phone
  • Zero stress on arrival
🛬 Airport SIM
  • China Mobile/Unicom counters at major airports
  • Bring your passport — required by law
  • ¥100–200 for 30–90 day tourist SIM
📡 Pocket WiFi
  • Rent at airport or pre-book online
  • Good for sharing with travel companions
  • Return at end of trip
💡 K's pick: eSIM before you fly. China Mobile has the best rural coverage.
💳 Payments in China · 支付
China is almost entirely cashless — here's how to survive
The reality: Even tiny street food stalls only accept QR code payments. Set this up at home before you fly.
💳
Alipay 支付宝 — Priority #1
Now officially supports foreign Visa/Mastercard/Amex. Download, register, link your card. Used everywhere.
✅ Use the "International" version. Link your card in Settings → Bank Cards.
💬
WeChat Pay 微信支付
Built into WeChat. Also supports foreign cards. Set it up as a backup while you're setting up WeChat anyway.
✅ You'll need WeChat — set up the payment while you're at it.
💵
Cash (Backup)
Bring ¥500–1000 RMB for emergencies. Exchange at Bank of China for best rates.
⚠️ Notify your home bank before travel to avoid ATM blocks.
🌐 Internet & Social Media · 网络
The Great Firewall — what's blocked and how to stay connected
Why are apps blocked? China operates a national internet filter. Google, Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, YouTube are all blocked — it's government policy, not a glitch.
🚫
What doesn't work in China
Google, Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Twitter/X, Snapchat, Spotify, Netflix, most Western news sites.
⚠️ Even some hotel WiFis are filtered.
🔐
VPN — The Golden Rule
Download and TEST your VPN BEFORE flying. You cannot easily download VPN apps once in China. This is the #1 mistake first-timers make.
💡 K's picks: ExpressVPN (most reliable) + Astrill as backup. Free VPNs almost never work.
✅
What works instead
WeChat (messaging), Amap (maps), Dianping (food reviews), Weibo (social), Bilibili (video), Baidu (search).
✅ All Chinese apps work perfectly without a VPN.
⚖️ VPN use by tourists is a gray area. Millions use them every year without issue. Personal use only.
📱 Essential Apps · 必备APP
Download these 6 before you fly — they run your entire trip
💬
WeChat 微信 — Everything App
Messaging, payments, food ordering, maps. Without WeChat, China is very hard.
✅ Register with your phone number before arrival.
💳
Alipay 支付宝 — Pay for Everything
China's dominant payment app. Foreign cards accepted. Link your card before you land.
✅ Link Visa/Mastercard in Settings. Do this BEFORE you arrive.
🚗
Didi 滴滴 — China's Uber
Works in English. No language barrier. Much cheaper than traditional taxis.
✅ Set pickup and destination in English — app handles translation.
🗺️
Amap 高德地图 — Navigation
Google Maps doesn't work well in China. Amap has an English mode and works offline.
✅ Switch to English in Settings. Download city maps offline.
🍜
大众点评 Dianping — Where to Eat
China's Yelp + TripAdvisor. Every restaurant rated. Filter for English menus.
✅ Best way to find hidden local spots tourists miss.
🚆
Trip.com (携程) — Book Everything
Trains, flights, hotels in one English app. Much easier than 12306 for foreigners.
✅ Book high-speed rail here — most reliable for foreigners.
🔤 Also download Google Translate with the Chinese offline pack before flying. Pleco is the best dictionary — point your camera at any text.
🚄 Getting Around China · 交通
World's best rail, easy metro, and Didi for taxis
🚄 High-Speed Rail
  • World's best rail system — use it between cities
  • Beijing → Shanghai: 4.5 hours
  • Book on Trip.com with your passport number
  • Bring passport to collect tickets at station
🚇 Metro
  • Every major city has a modern cheap metro
  • Machines have English — easy to use
  • Scan WeChat/Alipay QR at gates
  • ¥3–10 per journey
🚗 Didi (Taxi App)
  • Like Uber — no language barrier
  • ¥20–40 for most city rides
  • Also: 曹操出行, 花小猪 (cheaper)
  • Never use unlicensed airport taxis!
✈️ Domestic Flights
  • Best for very long distances
  • Book on Trip.com — English support
  • Arrive 2 hours early
  • Budget options: Xiamen Air, Shenzhen Airlines
